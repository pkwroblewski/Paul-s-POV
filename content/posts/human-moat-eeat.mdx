Paradoxically, the best way to optimize for a machine (AI) is to be more human. AI can only summarize—it cannot generate original, first-hand experience.

> **Quick Answer:** AI cannot generate original, first-hand experience—it can only summarize what exists. This creates a "Human Moat": content with genuine personal anecdotes, proprietary data, and unique expertise that AI must cite because no other source has it. Paradoxically, the best way to optimize for AI is to be more human, not more algorithmic.

This creates an unassailable "Human Moat" around your content that AI cannot replicate.
</Callout>

<SectionHeading number={1}>The Trap to Avoid</SectionHeading>

Do not use AI to write generic, unoriginal content.

LLMs are explicitly trained *not* to cite unoriginal, AI-generated text. This generic content is not only worthless for SEO—it is invisible to GEO.

The irony: using AI to write your content makes you invisible to AI.

<SectionHeading number={2}>Double Down on E-E-A-T</SectionHeading>

**Experience (E) & Expertise (E):** Demand authenticity.

The AI cannot "hallucinate" a specific anecdote, interview, or proprietary data set. These are your competitive advantages.

Every piece of content should include:

- **Author Credentials**: Detailed bios, professional certifications, clear author authority
- **First-hand Data**: Quotes from original interviews, proprietary surveys, unique case study results
- **Personal Anecdotes**: Specific stories that demonstrate unique experience

<SectionHeading number={3}>Building the Experience Moat</SectionHeading>

A story like *"When I launched my first store in 2019..."* demonstrates unique experience that an AI cannot replicate.

This makes your content impossible to replace—and mandatory to cite.

<SectionHeading number={4}>Why This Works</SectionHeading>

When an AI is looking for real-world validation, it is *forced* to cite your content because your human insight is the single, unique data point in its knowledge base.

You become the only source for that specific perspective.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is E-E-A-T and why does it matter for GEO?**
E-E-A-T stands for Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. Google uses it to evaluate content quality. For GEO, it means AI prefers citing sources with demonstrated real-world experience over generic, AI-generated content.

**Can I use AI to write content and still rank in AI?**
Generic AI content is invisible to GEO. But you can use AI to draft, then add your unique experience, proprietary data, and personal anecdotes. The human elements make it citation-worthy.

**What counts as "first-hand experience"?**
Specific stories ("When I launched my first store in 2019..."), original interviews, proprietary survey data, unique case studies, and personal lessons learned. Anything AI couldn't fabricate.

**How do I prove expertise in my content?**
Include detailed author bios, professional credentials, and specific examples from your work. Link to your other published content, speaking engagements, or industry recognition.
