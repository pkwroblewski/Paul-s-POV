The key is entity optimization: ensure AI understands *what* your business is (using structured data and consistent citations), not just what keywords you use.

<SectionHeading number={2}>Focus on Entity Optimization</SectionHeading>

**What is an Entity?**

An Entity is a "thing"—a person, business, product, or concept—that AI understands as a distinct, defined object.

**How to Optimize:**

- Use **structured data** (Schema Markup) to define your entity
- Ensure **consistent citation** across the web
- Build a **Human Moat** with E-E-A-T signals

The goal: ensure the AI understands *what* your business is—not just what keywords you use.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the difference between GEO and AEO?**
GEO focuses on getting cited in AI answers. AEO goes further: positioning your website as a trusted data source that AI queries directly. GEO is about visibility; AEO is about becoming essential infrastructure.

**What is an "entity" in the context of AEO?**
An entity is any defined "thing"—a person, business, product, or concept—that AI recognizes as distinct. Entity optimization means ensuring AI correctly understands and categorizes what you are.

**How do I optimize for entities?**
Use Schema Markup (structured data), maintain consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across the web, build triangulated authority through mentions, and clearly define your business on your website.

**When will AEO become mainstream?**
It's happening now in some industries. As AI becomes the primary interface for information retrieval, AEO will become as important as SEO was in the 2010s. Early movers will have significant advantages.
